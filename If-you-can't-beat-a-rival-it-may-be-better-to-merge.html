If you can't beat a rival, it may be better to merge.

Zero to One, Peter Thiel
1 min read ★

Peter Thiel started Confinity with his co-founder Max Levchin in 1998. When they released the Paypal product in late 1999. Elon Musk's X.com was right on their heels. X's product mirrored Paypal feature-for-feature. Infact the offices of both companies was four blocks apart on University Avenue in Palo Alto. By late 1999, both were out in war.

Many at Paypal logged 100-hour workweeks. No doubt that it was productive, but the focus wasn't on objective productivity, rather the focus was to defeat X.com.

But in February 2000, Elon and Peter both were scared about the rapidly inflating tech bubble, a financial crash would ruin them both.

So in early March they met in a cafe almost same distance away from their offices and negotiated a 50-50 merger. It killed the rivalry and as a unified team they were able to ride out the dot-com crash and then build a successful business.
